About

I recently graduated from the University of Oklahoma's Price College of Business with a Bachelor's in Sports Business & Analytics. I spent the past two seasons as a practice player with the Oklahoma women's basketball team, gaining hands-on experience inside a high-level college basketball program. That experience strengthened my passion for basketball analytics, film study, scouting, and defensive evaluation. At Oklahoma, I coded and built a custom defensive analytics dashboard and platform from scratch, developed a computer-vision pipeline using publicly available broadcasts, and completed a season-long defensive analysis report on Oklahoma's defensive possessions against top opponents. I am currently seeking a graduate assistant role for the 2026–27 season.

01 On Analytics

My view on analytics is that it's a small part of the puzzle. The goal isn't to redesign a system, but to give insight and find advantages in team building, playstyles, and in-game adjustments.

02 On the Game

I'm someone that is more process-oriented and looks to find ways to constantly learn about the game and a team. I enjoy reading about the game, studying different philosophies, and knowing what's important to a staff. Being a practice player has taught me as much about the importance of the intangibles as my work in analytics and coding.

03 On the Work

For every tool and research study, the goal is to adapt to the needs of the coaching staff. The right tools support a staff's philosophy and a team's development.
